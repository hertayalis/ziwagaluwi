AJ Ferrari has been making headlines for several days ever since he announced his availability for an NCAA team. While not all news about him is good, all news about him does have a relay point: Iowa Hawkeyes Wrestling. Although AJ has made it explicitly known that he wants to be a Hawkeye, no developments have been seen in this direction so far.

After several days of ambiguity, however, Iowa Hawkeyes Wrestling’s head coach Tom Brands has finally spoken regarding the matter. A social media post showcased the latest take of Brands on the matter. However, from what he said, things don’t look good for the 2021 NCAA wrestling champ.

AJ Ferrari’s wait not to end anytime soon?

FloWrestling recently added a video to its official X handle. The video carried a brief section of an interview with Tom Brands. The interviewer asked, “Do you have any wrestlers joining the roster for the second semester?” Answering the question, Brands said, “We do not. At this moment.” This comes as a wrecking ball on the hopes of the NCAA Wrestling champ. 

AJ Ferrari had officially visited several universities, with the University of Iowa being his final stop. His further social media updates almost guaranteed his acceptance into the wrestling program, especially since his brother Angelo has already committed to Iowa. But it seems that he will not be joining the Hawkeyes now.

Things were going smoothly for the 197-pounder till he got into a fight with Iowa’s Zach Glazier. While the fight started verbally, it ended up with AJ flipping double birdies at Glazier. Tom Brands was very firm and curt with his response. However, he did split his sentences and mentioned ‘at this moment’ separately. Does this mean that there is still a chance for AJ Ferrari to join? 

Loss of another year looms on 2021 NCAA Champ

While nothing is certain about the position of Iowa at the moment, AJ will need a clear answer or an NCAA team very soon. Because, if he doesn’t join a team soon, he may have to lose out on another year. This will add to his frustration since he hasn’t competed since 2022 after being ruled out due to a big injury.
