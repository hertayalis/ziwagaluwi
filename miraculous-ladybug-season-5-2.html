Who created the miraculous Ladybug?

Before we go into detail about what Miraculous Ladybug actually is, it’s important to understand the circumstances that led to the show’s conception. French author Thomas Astruc was the original creator of the show’s concept. When he was working on another popular animated children’s show, W.I.T.C.H., he was motivated by the woman who was wearing a t-shirt with a picture of a ladybug on it.

He then made the decision to produce an animated superhero show with a ladybug theme. Astruc met Jeremy Zang, who wanted to animate the program in the style of a cartoon even though he had no prior experience in the cartoon industry.

The creators wanted to make a cool superhero character with a European flair because the show was set in Paris. Although it was decided that the series would be animated in 3D, Toei Animation Company, a well-known Japanese company, joined the Miraculous Ladybug project as co-producers in 2012. The studio worked on another project with a Parisian setting before becoming intrigued by the Miraculous Ladybug project.

As a result of everyone’s excitement, the first Miraculous Ladybug 2D animation art concepts were produced and released, and they were an overwhelming success. However, issues with marketability and difficulties animating and drawing the Ladybugs’ all-black costume ultimately won out.

The show’s creators continued to use 3D animation, but Japanese colleagues continued to work on the project as co-producers. The project was initially developed in CGI by SAMG Animation, a Korean animation studio, and it has remained that way to this day.

The look of the show is somewhat reminiscent of anime, and the colors, camera angles, and even the details of the characters, like their transformations into their superhero persona, make anime a huge part of the Miraculous Ladybug project, despite the fact that the show is animated using CGI.

Sailor Moon, a well-known anime that tells the tale of Sailor Soldiers attempting to defend the world, was also created by the Toei Animation Company. One of the most recognizable features of the program is their transformations, which Miraculous Ladybug “borrowed” from Sailor Moon.
